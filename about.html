About the founder

Jennifer Starrett, MBA

More than a certificate. A confident bond for life.

Before Jennifer built a consulting practice, she built a plan for three sons who needed three completely different ones, while raising them on her own.

The proof

Three sons. Three different paths. One roadmap-builder.

Jennifer has been a single mother raising her three sons on her own for almost ten years, while teaching high school math full-time. Every family she works with is getting the same person who did this at her own kitchen table first.

Oldest, 24

The student who looked fine on paper, but needed help finding fit

Jennifer's oldest son was always a strong student, but didn't know where he wanted to go. Jennifer took him on countless college tours and made countless calls to admissions offices to track down the right fit, then did the diligent work on FAFSA, scholarships, and finding the right opportunities to make it affordable. He graduated from a top Texas university in 2024, Summa Cum Laude and on the Dean's List, and now works as a civil engineer in Austin, in a job he loves.

Middle, 22

The student who chose the expected path, then found his own

Jennifer's middle son enrolled at a four-year university for engineering, a path that was laid out for him rather than one he'd chosen. After two years, he realized it wasn't his major or his future, and that his real passion was music and business. Rather than treating that as a setback, Jennifer devised a new plan, contacting different schools and gathering the resources and schedules to get him back on his feet. He is now finishing an Associate's degree in Business and moving on to complete his Bachelor's at another university.

Youngest, 20

The student who didn't think he had a path at all

Jennifer's youngest son came into college with hearing loss and real doubts about his own direction. Today he's a UT Austin student and a multi-business owner, someone who describes himself as a driven pathfinder who loves learning everything under the sun. He'll be the first to say he couldn't have gotten anywhere near this level of clarity without his mother's guidance.

Beyond the classroom

7+ years of building plans for students everyone else had run out of ideas for

Jennifer's 7+ year career teaching high school math at an award-winning Austin-area high school has never been just about the curriculum. It's included building individualized learning plans and tracking real progress over time, helping students get on a real plan to graduate, and being the person a student turns to when a subject other than math isn't clicking either.

She's also brought real-world financial and accounting literacy into that work, helping students prepare for job applications and understand personal finance long before their first paycheck.

A track record before the business had a name

Long before Starrett Success Center existed, Jennifer was already doing this work informally, for her own sons, for their friends, and for neighbors who asked for help. Students and families she's worked with describe it the same way: not just a certificate at the end, but a confident bond for life.

That track record is what this business was built to formalize, and to offer beyond just the neighborhood she already knows.

Credentials

A finance background most college consultants don't have

Jennifer holds an MBA in Finance from the Kelley School of Business at Indiana University (3.9 GPA) and a BS in Business from Purdue University (3.8 GPA). Earlier in her career in financial services, she was recognized as an Employee of the Month and received a company award for building an online trade-review process that saved her employer more than $1 million a year.

Before teaching, she also built and grew businesses of her own, taking a professional chef services company from startup to profitable within its first year, managing 20+ employees and full design-build operations for multi-million dollar custom home projects, and leading construction teams that built 55 homes across four Austin-area neighborhoods as a top monthly producer.

Alongside her classroom work, Jennifer has long served as an active college and career booster for her special education students well past graduation day, staying involved as they figure out their next steps long after the diploma is handed out.
